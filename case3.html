Gaming Overload

In this painting the artist creates a surreal ambience which represents a world with humans and robots being interdependent and living together. The painting reflects the artist's idea that humans and robots can work together in a harmonious way and that the two can be complementary to each other. The painting is composed of various geometric shapes which create a sense of order and stability. The colours are muted and calming, giving the impression of a peaceful world where humans and robots live together in harmony.

Regarding social robots and human robots interaction, Christoph Lutz, a researcher at BI Norwegian Business School Oslo, who specializes investigate digital inequalities, online participation, trust, privacy and the sharing economy, raises "an important question in that regard is whether social robots can not only endanger users' privacy but potentially preserve or even enhance it". This ideology is evident in the context of the painting, it could be said that the artist is suggesting that social robots have the potential to help humans preserve their privacy, by following social rules and behaviors. This could be seen as a positive aspect of the robots, as they would be able to help humans maintain their privacy in a world where it is becoming increasingly difficult to do so.

Furthermore, the artist also presents a new perspective of the future which is different than flying cars and skyscrapers, rather a castle-looking civilization and people dressed like from the medieval age. The artist wants to give a message that the future is not about the technological advancements but about the way humans will interact with each other and with robots.
