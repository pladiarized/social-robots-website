Forgotten and Unwanted

This is the second artwork out of six piece collection titled "Forgotten and Unwanted". Here the artist amplifies the sound of silence, which surrounds the future lifestyle. We often think of the future as a utopia with sky-high building and "futuristic" technologies, whereas that vision is very biased to the western world. The artist wants the viewer to question the idea of the future, especially in a time where we have to question even basic things like clean water and air. Social robots, AI and other technologies that we haven't even thought of, are all part of the future, but what will we be left with when we lose our humanity?

The artist has used a limited palette to create a sense of emptiness and loneliness. The figure in the center is the only source of light and life in the painting, and it is surrounded by a dark, cold world. This figure represents the human race while the dark world around it represents the dangers and challenges that we will face in the future. This artist doesn't explicitly paint a robot or a unfamiliar piece of technology yet the smoke, old crooked building and trash lying on the ground presents a new perspective into the future.

"At the robotic moment, more than ever, our willingness to engage with the inanimate does not depend on being deceived but on wanting to fill in the blanks." This thought is presented in Turkle's book "Alone Together" which aligns with the headspace of the artist. As presented, too much dependency could pave way for a future will be a time when we will be living in our own heads, with technology as our only source of communication. This will lead to a loss of human connection and empathy.
